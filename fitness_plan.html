How This Works

  • Strength training builds muscle, raising your resting metabolism

  • High protein keeps you full and protects muscle while losing fat
  • You're already getting stronger — the food side unlocks the weight loss

☕ Coffee & Caffeine
Pre-Workout (best use)
Black coffee 30–60 min before your workout is a free performance booster. Pair with creatine gummies for a solid pre-workout.
Keep Calories Low
Black coffee = 0 kcal. A splash of unsweetened oat/almond milk adds ~15 kcal. Avoid flavored syrups.
Cut Off at 2pm
Caffeine has a 5–6 hr half-life. Late coffee disrupts sleep, raises cortisol, and makes weight loss harder.
Afternoon Alternative
Matcha or green tea after 12pm — lower caffeine, L-theanine gives calm focus without the jitters.
Watch out: Coffee suppresses appetite in the morning — great in theory, but leads to under-eating early and overeating at night. Have breakfast within an hour of waking even if you're not hungry.

💜 Low Motivation Days
The 10-Minute RuleTell yourself you'll just do the warm-up and first two exercises. Most of the time you'll keep going. If not, 2 exercises beats zero.
The Minimum WorkoutOn truly bad days: Push = shoulder press + lateral raise. Pull = hammer curls + seated row. Legs = squats + glute bridge. Done. Valid.
Food is Your Biggest LeverWorking out 3× a week burns maybe 400–600 extra calories total. One good week of eating creates more deficit than two weeks of workouts. Batch prep Sunday is non-negotiable.
What to Track Instead of the ScaleThe scale doesn't tell the full story. Track: how your clothes fit, reps vs last month, energy at 3pm, how you feel after workouts.
Pottery CountsUpper body, grip strength, core engagement — it counts. Pottery + one workout = two training sessions. Not a bad week.
Use the Cold Meals on Hard DaysThe marinated salads require zero effort to serve. Grab the chickpea salad or orzo straight from the fridge. You batch-prepped it exactly for this moment.
